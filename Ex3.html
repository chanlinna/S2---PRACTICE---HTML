Blog

The moon is Earth's only natural satellite
and has captivated human curiosity for centuries.
Its gravitational pull influences tides,
and it plays a crucial role in Earth's life cycles.

The moon's surface is marked by craters
from ancient impacts, which help scientists
understand the history of our solar system.

The moon has also been a symbol in many cultures,
representing time, mythology, and inspiration
for countless people around the world.
